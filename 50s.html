Influential People

First person accounts of the 10 most influential people of the 50s.


Fidel Castro

Equality in Cuba is necessary. I’ve done good, I’ve done bad, and I’ve made a change for better or for worse. People now have better health care and housing in Cuba. Yet, the citizens still feel the need to have word in the government. This shall not happen for I am the supreme leader of this country. More and more arrests shall be made against my own people for they do not respect my power. I used to preach open speech, yet now I despise it. My people need to know what I can do.



Queen Elizabeth II

I have been beat down as a horrible person since birth. Being a good person, I persevere through my troubles truthfully and nobly, proving innocence of any wrongdoings in my early years. Now, through ups and downs, I have become the leader of one of the world’s superpowers. I have led multiple defenses of raids from foreign countries. I have gained respect from my own and even more respect from people in other countries. I have spread the religion of protestantism and have become the best Queen of Great Britain.





Martin Luther King Jr.

My people have never been more oppressed, yet with my help, they have gained some freedom. I have won the peace prize last year in 1964, this has shown a person of any race can do anything they put their mind to. Through the help help of the African American community standing up for the truth, we have come a far ways. People have seemed to love my dream speech in 1963, which have started a revolution for equality. I hope more and more people stand up for what is right, as I feel my life will be drawn short soon.



Gandhi

I have fought for racial justice in South Africa, I have been successful in my protests. I preach the peace in getting things done. I have seen my people be discriminated in India by the British rule. One of my greatest achievements is protesting the British goods like clothes to show them the Indian people have power too. They do not deserve to run our country. I have successfully had the British leave my country and I have shown the world what I can do with the help of my people.






Nikita Khrushchev

I’ve led the Soviets during the height of the Cold War. I’ve pursued peaceful coexistence with the west. I am a communist by heart. I officially destabilized the Soviet Union as he messed everything up with our reputation. I was leader of the Soviet Union during the space race, and I have instigated the Cuban missile crisis by putting weapons 90 miles from the coast of Florida. I have made an important impact on the Soviet’s in both positive and negative ways.



Kim Il-sung

I have made North Korea flourish in communism after the Korean war. I have set forth in the Juche system in North Korea to emphasize political, economic, and military independence. I started the production of North Korea’s nuclear bomb production. This shows North Korea is not to be messed with and will not be taken lightly. I have made my people of North Korea believe I am a God, they hang pictures of me in their houses and clean them every day.





Dr. Seuss

I have been considered my people to be the most influential children’s book writer in the world. I have received many rewards for my life work. During WWII, I served as head of the animation department of the First Motion Picture Unit of the United States Air Forces. After the World War, I helped ease the minds of younglings by creating fun rhyming stories to also help them read. These stories encouraged kids to read more and have fun with it.

Important Events

The 10 most important events of the 50s.

1950

December 1952

The city of London was shut down due to a dense toxic smog that reduced visibility to only a few feet. This is the worst air pollution crisis in Europe to this day and it killed 8,000 to 12,000 people and around 4,000 babies died prematurely. Following the mass tragedy, Parliament passed The Clean Air Act of 1956 and prevented the burning of coal around an urban area and was the first step towards clean energy efforts in Europe.

1953

March 1953

American medical researcher Dr. Jonas Salk announced that he successfully created and tested a vaccine for Polio. Polio has affected humanity throughout history and can cause paralysis. The disease became widespread across the world in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. After the first year it was available, the number of Polio cases dropped severely and today there is only a handful of cases reported each year.
